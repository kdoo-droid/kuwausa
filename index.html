Japanese Source

Why Japanese Mulberry
Is Different

Kuwanosato is located in Ichikawa Mishima Town, Yamanashi Prefecture — the birthplace of the Ichinose mulberry variety (一瀬桑), first recognized in 1916. That cultivar became so exceptional that over 90% of Japan's silkworm farmers adopted it nationwide, and it is today designated a Yamanashi Prefecture natural monument. Kuwanosato grows exclusively this heritage variety, on the same land where it was born — fields with over a century of unbroken mulberry farming history.

Japan's mulberry heritage runs deep through sericulture — the tradition of raising silkworms for silk, including the imperial custom of the Empress personally tending mulberry leaves. Kuwanosato operates from the former agricultural cooperative building where silk cocoons were once collected, a physical connection to that era. When Japan's silk industry declined post-WWII, those mulberry fields were abandoned. Kuwanosato revived them — carrying over a century of agricultural tradition forward for modern wellness.

Wellness Profile

The Science Behind
Mulberry Wellness

Mulberry leaves contain a range of naturally occurring compounds that have attracted growing interest in nutrition and wellness research. Five key nutrients found in whole-leaf mulberry — DNJ, GABA, flavonoids, calcium and magnesium, and dietary fiber — are described below in clear, credible language.

DNJ

Mulberry leaf naturally contains DNJ, a compound studied for supporting healthy carbohydrate metabolism and helping moderate how the body processes sugars after meals.

GABA

Mulberry leaf naturally contains GABA, a compound associated with calm and balance in the body.

Antioxidants

Mulberry leaf contains naturally occurring flavonoids and polyphenols, including rutin, quercetin-family compounds, and chlorogenic acid, which help support the body's antioxidant defenses.

Minerals & Fiber

Mulberry leaf also contributes naturally occurring minerals and fiber, making it more than a single-compound ingredient. It is a broad-spectrum plant wellness ingredient.

Trust & Provenance

Exclusively Produced by
Kuwanosato, Japan

The mulberry you receive carries over a century of Japanese heritage. The Ichinose variety grown by Kuwanosato has been cultivated in Ichikawa Mishima Town, Yamanashi since 1916 — the same fields, the same mountain terroir that once supplied Japan's national silk industry. This is not a new crop. It is a recovered treasure.

Kuwanosato is the dedicated steward of that heritage. Founded in 2004, the company cultivates over 45,000 mulberry trees across 10+ hectares at 700 meters elevation with no pesticides or herbicides. A 100,000-tree new planting project with local Yamanashi authorities ensures the tradition continues for future generations.
